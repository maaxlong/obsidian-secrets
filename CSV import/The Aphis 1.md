**BFI synopsis:** A film showing the features and reproduction of Aphides. (Synopsis) The life cycle of the rose aphid (Macrosiphum Roseus). BBFC certificate, main title and production credits (47). Aphid on rosebud (86). Aphids feeding by driving beak into stem and drawing up sap (94). Microscopic shot to show beak (125). CU aphid on stem. Commentary explains that the sap is digested by insect and made into a sugary syrup, honeydew, which it discharges through two tubes on its back (173). Ants eating the honeydew by stroking the aphid (200). Ant on rose leaf pulling the legs of another insect in rivalry for the honeydew (224). Aphids on flower stem. Commentary explains that aphid produces young every 90 minutes (277). Young aphid shedding first coat of membrane (295). Young aphids clustered on stem. Commentary explains that they are all the result of one aphid reproducing over 24 hours (312). The stem after a week, clustered with aphids (406). The aphids three weeks later. The commentary states that the aphids all come from the same mother and the mother is indicated (357). Fly larvae on stem eating aphids (397). Larvae of ladybird eating aphids (416). CU ladybird eating aphid. Commentary explains that the ladybird sucks dry the aphid and does not chew it (455). Fully grown aphid showing wings; the wings opening (561). Aphids on a new rose shoot [to start a new colony] (606). Black aphids on dock leaves; the leaves curling. Commentary explains that they curl because of the irritation of the weight of the aphids. The curled leaf protects the aphids (665). The underside of a dock leaf clustered with aphids (679). A single wingless male (696). Male and female aphids on a stem mating (732). The aphid eggs and aphids feeding from the leaf (758). The torpedo-shaped eggs (774). A gardener spraying his bean plants; CU leaves being sprayed (797). Gardener spraying the roots of his roses; CU rose bush and leaves being sprayed (832). Logo (839ft). Note: Main credits cite `Pro Patria Limited presents'. (Shotlist)
